New

The New Automated Agency (NAU) is a student-led design think-tank that pilots interactive digital experiments to investigate the consequences of automation-assisted employment.

In the face of rising usage and reliance on automation, NAU aims to raise awareness and enable others to reclaim some of the agency that automation takes away from us.

All names, characters, places, events, locales, and incidents in the scenarios are either the products of NAU’s imagination or used in a fictitious manner. Any resemblance to actual persons, living or dead, or actual events is purely coincidental.

The Present

The pandemic leads to mass layoffs. As the economy starts to recover slowly, applicants start reapplying for jobs en-masse. HR departments are overwhelmed.

Demand for fast, efficient processing of applications leads to sped up adoption and unrestrained use of AI technologies, at every step of the hiring process.

“We regret

You are one of those affected by the pandemic layoffs. Lately, your mailbox has been packed with automated rejection responses. None of them tell you what went wrong.

You are suitably concerned with the new developments.

News headlines call for more transparency and public accountability. As a response, the EU begins actioning legislative reforms for AI usage and human rights.

As you submit an application for yet another job, you check a beta feature promising detailed feedback. Couple of weeks later, your inbox ‘pings’ to let you know you didn’t get it. Dammit. However, this email looks a little different. You start reading...

Would you rely on this tool?

A. Yes

You get overly reliant on the tool. As a result, your applications look increasingly generic. You often get past the automated screening systems. But the human recruiters? Not so much.

B. No

Several other applicants start using it to optimize their resumes too. Eventually, you are forced to do the same just to keep up.

A Possible Tomorrow

Job seekers slowly start understanding how the algorithms work. Faced with high competition and task-automation in hiring – there is an accelerated trend of digital self-optimization, for better machine legibility.

The Way Forward

The journey through these scenarios are intended as little windows of understanding and engagement with –the diverse possibilities and complex futures of– automated recruitment. We hope that this project triggers a plurality of public reactions leading towards concrete actions, be it caution, protest or hope.
