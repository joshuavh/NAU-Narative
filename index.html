The New Automated Agency (NAU) is a student-led design think-tank that pilots interactive digital experiments to investigate the consequences of automation-assisted employment.
In the face of rising usage and reliance on automation, NAU aims to raise awareness and enable people to reclaim some of the agency that automation takes away from us.

The Present

The pandemic leads to mass layoffs. As the economy starts to recover slowly, applicants start reapplying for jobs en-masse. HR departments are overwhelmed. Demand for fast, efficient processing of applications leads to sped up adoption of AI technologies in recruitment.

“We regret
   to inform you...”

You are one of those affected by the pandemic layoffs. Lately, your mailbox has been packed with automated rejection responses. None of them tell you what went wrong.

The EU begins actioning legislative reforms for AI usage and human rights. There are news features that call for more transparency and accountability.

As you submit an application for yet another job, you check a beta feature promising 'detailed feedback'. Couple of weeks later, your inbox ‘pings’ to let you know you didn’t get the job. This email however, looks a little different...

A Possible Tomorrow

People slowly start understanding how the algorithms work. In the face of growing competition and automation in HR, there is an accelerated trend of optimizing for machine legible CVs.

Incognito CV
